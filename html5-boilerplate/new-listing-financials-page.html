INSTRUCTIONS

You can either list your company asking for investment, or not ask for investment. If you ask for investment, then investors from around the world can bid on your company. Only investors accredited in their jurisdiction are allowed to legally invest, so you will only receive bids from accredited investors.

Upload your business plan, a short investor-oriented presentation, and your latest financial statements. These need not be audited, and you can exclude any information which is of a private or trade secret nature. As most professional investors review a large number of companies each day, be brief and concise in your documents.

Note that if you are asking for money, you are subject to anti-fraud provisions. Speficially, you must be honest and not withold any material information concerning your business to investors. Failure to do so could subject you to legal consequences, up to and including criminal charges and imprisonment. Startupbidder will report suspected fraudulent behavior to the relevant authorities and will fully cooperate in any investigations that may result.

TIPS

ASKING - amount of money you want for investment, between $5,000 and $500,000 USD

FOR - what percentage of common equity you are selling in exchange for the investment, between 5% and 50%

AS - what kind of investment do you prefer: pure common equity, convertible note, or preferred stock?

AT - what interest rate you want to pay on your liability, only applies to convertible note or preferred stock

BY - end date for your bidding between 30 and 90 days in the future

PLAN - your business plan, a Word or PDF document that describes your business in detail

SLIDE DECK - your presentation to investors, a Powerpoint or PDF document, keep it short

FINANCIALS - your most current financial statements, if you have no operating history, then list current capital and runway
