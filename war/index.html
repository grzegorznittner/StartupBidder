FAQ - Frequently Asked Questions
1.
What is Startupbidder?
A crowdsourced startup company information site. On it you can place your business plan, comment on business plans, up or down vote a business plan, and if you're an investor, bid for a stake in the company!
2.
Who runs Startupbidder?
Greg Nittner and John Arley Burns, two guys who wanted to bring more visibility to the otherwise opaque world of startup business plan valuation.
3.
How can I contact you?
See the contact page for more detail.
4.
What is the algorithm you use to rank business plans? 
Used by search for the 'top listings' query:

p = votes from users
b = number of bids
c = number of comments
v = median valuation (normalized / $100,000)
t = time since submission in days
s = score

putting it together:

s = (p + b + c + v) / (t + 2)^1.5

Reference:
seomoz.org
4.
What happens when a user accepts a bid?
When the owner of the listing accepts a bid, the lister and the users who made the bid are then notified via email of the bid acceptance. The lister will then be billed a an acceptance charge of 0.5% of the funding amount. For instance, funding of $10,000 would have an acceptance charge of $50. It is then the responsibility of the lister to follow up with the member for a formal legal funding agreement.
5.
Are you offering shares in these companies for sale?
No. Startupbidder is an informational site linking startup entpreneurs with investors. We are not offering shares for sale or promoting any shares via this site. All investments must be concluded between the parties themselves with proper legal representation.
6.
What is the algorithm you use to rank users? 
Ranking is similar as to that done for the 'top listings' query, except from a user standpoint:

p = votes from other users
b = number of bids placed
c = number of comments in total
v = median valuation of bids (normalized / $100,000)
t = time since last activity in days
s = score

putting it together:

s = (p + b + c + v) / (t + 2)^1.5
